After my graduation from KU, I started working as a software engineer for a GRC company named Lockpath in Overland Park, KS. Initially I was assigned to the back-end team, but after six months I was recruited to the front-end to assist with a critical upgrade project. It was during this time that I realized I loved working on front-end projects. Angular and Typescript became my bread and butter, but as the years went on I was positioned as a full-stack engineer to support the ASPX-to-Angular conversion projects that were my focus.

In 2019, Lockpath was bought by a company based in Portland called NAVEX Global. Lockpath's flagship web application, Keylight, was renamed to Lockpath and became an official NAVEX offering. Three years later, a rebrand resulted in the "Global" suffix being dropped from the company name and Lockpath was renamed to NAVEX IRM.

During my sophomore year of college I accepted a position as a part-time web developer for AllofE Solutions. The flexibility of the job allowed me to continue classes while gaining real development experience in a professional environment.
